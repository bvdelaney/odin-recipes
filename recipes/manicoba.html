Manicoba

Amazonian cassava leaves stew

Manicoba is a traditional Amazonian dish very popular in the north of Brazil. It has recently gained attention for a peculiar reason: it can be poisonous if prepared incorrectly. Despite the danger this recipe is widely consumed in the country and it's delicious. The prep time is around a week and it serves about 35-40 people.

Ingredients

  • 15kgs of fresh cassava/mandioca leaves
  • 2kgs of lard
  • 2kgs of smoked bacon, whole
  • 2kgs of pig's feet, salted
  • 2kgs of pig's ear, salted
  • 2kgs of pig's tongue, salted
  • 2kgs of pork loin, salted
  • 2kgs of pork ribs, salted
  • 4kgs of carne de sol
  • 1.5kgs of kielbasa-type sausage
  • 1.5kgs of chorizo sausage
  • 1.5kgs of linguica sausage
  • 4.5kgs of beef tripe

Steps

  1. Remove stalks and central vein from cassava leaves.
  2. Throughly wash them in cold water.
  3. Grind the leaves using the fines blade of a meat grinder - you should have approximately 6kgs of pulped leaves.
  4. Put the cassava leaves in a 40-50 quart industrial stockpot, add water and let it simmer for 3 days.
  5. Throughly mix 8-12 times a day, making sure the leaves don't stick to the bottom.
  6. Add water to the mixture when necessary to avoid drying out.
  7. After 3 days, add the lard and the smoked bacon.
  8. Cook it for another 24 hours repeating the same process of adding water and mixing it.
  9. On the fourth day, in another pot, add all the meats with the execption of the sausages and the tripe and cover with cold water.
  10. Soak for 24 hours, changing the water several times.
  11. On the fifth day, chop the trip and pour boiling water over it to scald it, and let it cool in the water.
  12. Remove the salted meats and the tripe from their soaking water, wash them throughly and boil them for 1 hour.
  13. Add the salted meats and the tripe to the cassava leaf mixture and cook for another 2 days, removing the pot from the heat while sleeping.
  14. On the sixth day, cut all the sausages into thick roups, then add them to the stockpot.
  15. Cook it for another 24 hours
  16. On the seventh day the manicoba is ready to serve, usually accompanied by white rice.
